## Summary
In April 2025, the SEC Division of Corporation Finance issued guidance on disclosure requirements for crypto asset securities offerings. The guidance covers Form S-1, Form 10, Form 20-F, and Form 1-A (Reg A), providing issuers clarity on what must be disclosed about networks, tokens, governance, and risks.

## Key Facts
- SEC Corp Fin issued guidance April 10, 2025 amid Crypto Task Force deliberations (Acting Chairman Uyeda)
- Covers offerings under Securities Act 1933 and Exchange Act 1934
- Key disclosure areas:
  - **Business description**: Current stage of development, token function in network, milestone timelines
  - **Token characteristics**: Rights conferred, role in network/application, supply mechanics
  - **Technology**: Consensus mechanism, block size, gas fees, open-source vs proprietary
  - **Governance**: Decentralization timeline, who controls the protocol post-launch
  - **Risk factors**: Regulatory uncertainty, technical risks, key-person risk
- Guidance applies to issuers registering securities under Act or qualifying under Reg A
- Disclosure should be consistent with public statements, whitepapers, and developer docs
- Scaled disclosure permitted for smaller issuers

> [!fact] Official SEC source, April 2025
> This is the most current SEC staff guidance on crypto securities disclosure as of the research date.

> [!analysis] The guidance signals the SEC is moving toward clearer (not lighter) disclosure standards rather than carving out crypto from securities laws. Issuers cannot hide behind technical jargon.

## How it relates to Logos
- Any Logos ecosystem project conducting a US-registered or Reg A token offering must follow this guidance
- The requirement to disclose decentralization timeline is particularly relevant — projects claiming utility token status must demonstrate credible path to decentralization
- Whitepaper consistency requirement means marketing materials and technical docs must align
